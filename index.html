About Me

About

Hi, I am Junying. I am a PhD candidate in Statistics at Stony Brook University under supervision by Dr. Jie Yang and Dr. Song Wu. My research interests include early phases adaptive clinical trial designs, machine learning, and deep learning. I have experience as a Research Assistant (Biostatistician) at Biostatistical Consulting Core at Stony Brook Medicine for 4 years and Data Scientist intern at Genentech for 1 year.

Skills

  • Programming Languages: Python, R, SAS, SQL, Matlab, C/C++
  • Industry Knowledge: Adaptive Clinical Trial Design, Machine Learning, Deep Learning, Reinforcement Learning, Recommendation System, System Design, A/B Testing, Casual Inference, Time Series, Survival Analysis, Longitudinal Analysis, Data Manipulation, Data Structure and Algorithm
  • Personal Interests: Swimming, Skiing, Snowboarding, Free Diving, Badminton

Research Interests

My research interests focus on designing advanced early phases clinical trials to accelerate the trial process, improve the accuracy of selecting the right dose or dose combination, and stopping early if safety or futility issue occur. We recently published an R package on the R CRAN website to execute the whole process of seamless phase I/II adaptive drug combination designs based on Bayesian inference using Continual Resessment Method (CRM).
