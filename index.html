About Me

About

Hi, I am Junying. I am a PhD candidate in Statistics at Stony Brook University under the supervision of Dr. Jie Yang and Dr. Song Wu. My research interests include early phases adaptive clinical trial designs, machine learning, and deep learning. I have experience as a Research Assistant (Biostatistician) at Biostatistical Consulting Core at Stony Brook Medicine for 4 years and as a Data Scientist intern at Genentech for 1 year.

Research Interests

My research interests focus on designing advanced early phase clinical trials to accelerate the trial process, improve the accuracy of selecting the right dose or dose combination, and stopping early if safety or futility issues occur. We recently published an R package on the R CRAN website to execute the whole process of seamless phase I/II adaptive drug combination designs based on Bayesian inference using the Continual Reassessment Method (CRM).

Research & Projects

Research: Dose optimization strategies for early phases clinical trials

  • crm12Comb: Phase I/II Adaptive Design for Drug Combinations based on CRM Design through R

    Executed the entire design process using R, and generated a user-friendly R package crm12Comb published on the R CRAN website for clinical researchers to simulate and implement the designs using the Bayesian framework with flexible inputs, including link functions and prior distributions, allowing adaptability for different clinical datasets.

    crm12Comb

  • Explored advanced algorithms for early-phase adaptive clinical trial designs, aimed at reducing the sample size, allocating more patients to optimal drug regimens, or stopping trials early for safety or futility.
  • During my data science intern at Genentech, our team developed three advanced simulation algorithms for dose optimization using R, improving dose selection efficiency and accuracy across diverse clinical scenarios. Techniques include 3+3 design, BOIN design, Emax model, logistic regression model, backfill strategies, and randomized expansion cohorts. We further deployed an R Shiny app with customized inputs, and allowed parameter adjustments to support clinical scientists with real-time, data-driven decision guidance.
